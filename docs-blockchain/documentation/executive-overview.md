# Executive Overview

## Executive Overview

> **a brief overview of the material to be found in the remainder of the document**

`[3-5 paragraphs]`

**audience**:&#x20;

`non-technical busy executives: stakeholders and decision makers`

`few minutes read`&#x20;

#### Content

a brief overview of&#x20;

* the problem being addressed
* the proposed solution
* proposed solutions' intended benefits
* proposed solutions' potential risks



address each of the following areas briefly

* ﻿﻿**Introduction**: introduction to the&#x20;
  * purpose of the document&#x20;
  * the problem or opportunity that the proposed solution addresses
* **Objectives**&#x20;
  * main objectives of the solution — should be clear
    * what problem does it aim to solve | what goals does it seek to achieve
* **Scope:** scope of the solution
  * areas or processes it covers&#x20;
  * limitations or exclusions
* **Key Benefits:** key benefits and outcomes of implementing the solution
  * how will it positively impact the organization, stakeholders, or processes
* **High-Level Description**
  * concise & non-technical overview of the solution's&#x20;
    * architecture
    * components&#x20;
    * technologies involved
  * avoid technical jargon, acronyms, abbreviations
  * focus on conveying the solution's structure and flow
* **Key Features**
  * highlight the most important features and functionalities that the solution offers
  * focus on those aspects that set the solution apart or contribute significantly to its success
  * tell the busy executive why what you're doing is so important and why it should really matter to them
* **Approach**
  * briefly explanation
  * the approach or methodology that will be used to implement the solution
    * development methodologies
    * project management frameworks
    * any unique approaches taken
    * solution deliverable techniques
* **Timeline**: high-level timeline
  * an estimate of the project timeline
    * when project starts&#x20;
    * major milestones &#x20;
    * key phases
    * when you plan to be able to go live
  * helps stakeholders understand the project's expected duration and overall level of effort&#x20;
* **Resource Requirements**
  * types of resources required for the solution's implementation&#x20;
    * personnel
    * technology: key technology components, systems
    * infrastructure&#x20;
    * funding
* **Risks and Mitigation**
  * identify potential risks or challenges associated with the solution's implementation &#x20;
  * outline the strategies planned to mitigate these risks
* **Alignment with Goals/Business Objectives**
  * explain how the proposed solution (technology decision) aligns with the organization's preexisting&#x20;
    * strategic goals
    * mission&#x20;
    * vision
  * do not let technological capability define business objectives&#x20;
    * craft goals or business objectives outside of any particular technology
* **Next Steps**
  * summarize the immediate next steps in the implementation process if you get executive approval
    * project initiation
    * stakeholder engagement
    * any required approvals
* **Conclusion**
  * Conclude the overview by reiterating&#x20;
    * the significance of the solution &#x20;
    * solutions' anticipated impact
