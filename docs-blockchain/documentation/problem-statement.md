# Problem Statement

## Problem Statement

> **clear description of the problems** (issues / challenges)&#x20;
>
> &#x20;      **the proposed solution aims to address**
>
> — subtle try to sell the value of the proposed solution by showing how problematic things are now —&#x20;

**purpose**: convey the reader why your idea matters; help readers understand why the problems you're trying to solve are important ones



#### Content

* **Introduction**: introduce the purpose of the section
  * a comprehensive overview of the problems or challenges that the project seeks to resolve
* **Background and Context**
  * background information about the project
  * its history
  * pertinent context that helps readers understand why the problems are significant and need to be addressed — that can not be captured elsewhere in the document
* **Current State Analysis**
  * describe current situation or environment in which the problems exist&#x20;
    * existing businesses processes
    * line of business
    * legacy systems
    * legacy technologies&#x20;
    * practices implemented across the organization that contribute to the problems
* **Challenges and Pain Points**
  * ﻿﻿clearly articulate the&#x20;
    * specific challenges
    * pain points&#x20;
    * inefficiencies t
    * general rough areas hat are affecting the current state
  * use following to illustrate the impact of challenges
    * quantitative data
    * examples
    * anecdotes from existing users/participants in the solution ecosystem
* **Scope Definition**
  * detailed definition of the scope of the problem statement by outlining the boundaries of the issues you intend to address
    * exactly what aspects of what problems are you aiming to solve&#x20;
    * which are you aiming to ignore at least with this current solution
  * helps prevent scope creep and ensures that the document remains focused on just delivering the business value you intend to deliver
* **Impact Assessment**
  * discuss the negative consequences/impacts of the identified challenges; potential costs of not addressing this problem
    * financial
    * operational
    * user experience
    * compliance
    * other&#x20;
* **Stakeholder Analysis**
  * ﻿﻿identify the stakeholders who are affected by or have a vested interest in resolving the identified problems
    * ﻿﻿end users
    * customers
    * employees
    * auditing/regulatory bodies&#x20;
    * partners
    * vendors
    * suppliers
    * distributors
    * resellers
    * others
* **Importance and Justification**
  * ﻿﻿explain why it's crucial to address these problems
  * ﻿﻿highlight the&#x20;
    * potential benefits
    * opportunities&#x20;
    * strategic importance of finding solutions to each problem
* **Limitations of Existing Solutions**
  * show why previous attempts to solve this problem have fallen short or been unsuccessful — outline the limitations or shortcomings of those solutions
  * helps set the stage for the innovative approach you're proposing
* **Future Risks and Consequences**
  * discuss the potential risks and negative outcomes if the problems are not addressed
    * potential loss of business
    * reputational damage
    * legal issues
    * competitive disadvantages
* **Desired Outcomes**
  * a high-level overview of the desired outcomes or goals that the proposed solution aims to achieve
    * improvements in efficiency
    * cost savings
    * customer satisfaction & retention
    * etc
