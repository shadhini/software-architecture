## Blockchain Regulations: European Union

There are number of direct and indirect regulations in effect in the EU.

<mark style="color:$primary;background-color:$primary;">**MiCA Regulation**</mark>

*   **`MiCA`: Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation**

    * regulation aimed at establishing a comprehensive framework for regulating **crypto-assets** and related services across member states



<mark style="color:$primary;background-color:$primary;">**AML: Anti-Money Laundering**</mark>

* **`5AMLD`: Fifth Anti-Money Laundering Directive**&#x20;
  * requires entities dealing with virtual currencies to conduct **customer due diligence** and report suspicious activities to law enforcement agencies â€” **`KYC`** practices
  * brings some **virtual currency** activities under anti-money laundering (**`AML`**) regulations&#x20;
  * require cryptocurrency business to register and implement `AML` and `KYC` practices



<mark style="color:$primary;background-color:$primary;">**Data Protection**</mark>

* **`GDPR`: General Data Protection Regulation**
  * went into effect in the EU in 2019
  * applies to the storage management and processing of **personal data** within the EU, including data stored on blockchains
  * sets guidelines for **data protection** and **individuals' rights** including the right to be forgotten



<mark style="color:$primary;background-color:$primary;">**Other**</mark>

* **`eIDAS` Regulation**
  * the electronic identification, authentication, and trust services regulation establishes a framework for **electronic signatures and trust services**
  * indirect regulation â€” may be relevant to blockchain-based documents
* **`PSD2`: Payment Services Directive 2**
  * PSD2 regulates **payment services** within the EU including cryptocurrency-related activities
* Prospectus Regulation
  * relevant to **security tokens** and **tokenized securities**
  * regulation provides guidelines for **issuing** [**prospectuses**](#user-content-fn-1)[^1] when offering securities to the public&#x20;

<mark style="color:$primary;background-color:$primary;">**Taxation**</mark>

* Each member state has its taxation rules for cryptocurrencies, making **cross-border compliance complex**.
