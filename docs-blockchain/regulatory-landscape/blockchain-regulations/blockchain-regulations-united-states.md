## Blockchain Regulations: United States

{% hint style="info" %}
Regulations in the United States are often implemented **verbatim** in other countries around the world.
{% endhint %}

* US law is made complex by the existence of government at multiple levels ‚Äî federal and state/local
* **Cryptocurrency** is **recognized on a federal level**, but laws may differ from state to state

{% hint style="warning" %}
While regulations&#x20;

* ‚úÖ exist around cryptocurrency,&#x20;
* ‚ùó very few exist around blockchain-based business models.
{% endhint %}





<mark style="color:$primary;background-color:$primary;">**Several federal agencies regulate blockchain-related business in the United States**</mark>

* **`SEC`**: The **Securities and Exchange Commission**&#x20;
  * defines **cryptocurrency** as a **security**
  * has released several statements defining tokens sold via Initial Coin Offerings (`ICO`s) as securities
  * has been actively regulating ICOs
  * [https://www.sec.gov/newsroom/speeches-statements/testimony-virtual-currencies-oversight-role-us-securities-exchange-commission-us-commodity-futures](https://www.sec.gov/newsroom/speeches-statements/testimony-virtual-currencies-oversight-role-us-securities-exchange-commission-us-commodity-futures)
* **`FinCEN`: Financial Crimes Enforcement Network**
  * concerned with combating money laundering and illicit financial activities&#x20;
  * it requires cryptocurrency exchanges and business to adhere to following regulations
    * **`AML`**: **Anti-Money Laundering**&#x20;
    * **`KYC`**: **Know Your Customer**
* **`CFTC`**: The **Commodities Futures Trading Commission**&#x20;
  * defines **cryptocurrency** as a **commodity**
  * [https://www.cftc.gov/sites/default/files/idc/groups/public/%40customerprotection/documents/file/oceo\_aivc0218.pdf](https://www.cftc.gov/sites/default/files/idc/groups/public/%40customerprotection/documents/file/oceo_aivc0218.pdf)
* **`FTC`**: The US **Federal Trade Commission**&#x20;
  * has created a **Blockchain Working Group** aimed at cracking down on **illegal** and **fraudulent** **schemes**
  * [https://www.ftc.gov/policy/advocacy-research/tech-at-ftc/2018/03/its-time-ftc-blockchain-working-group](https://www.ftc.gov/policy/advocacy-research/tech-at-ftc/2018/03/its-time-ftc-blockchain-working-group)



<mark style="color:$primary;background-color:$primary;">**State Level Regulation**</mark>

* some states have embraced blockchain and cryptocurrencies, while others have imposed stricter regulations or outright bans



<mark style="color:$primary;background-color:$primary;">**Taxation**</mark>

* cryptocurrency transactions are subject to taxation
* **`IRS`**: The **U.S. Internal Revenue Service**&#x20;
  * defines **cryptocurrency** as **assets for taxation purposes**
  * views digital currencies as property
  * [https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-drop/n-14-21.pdf](https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-drop/n-14-21.pdf)
