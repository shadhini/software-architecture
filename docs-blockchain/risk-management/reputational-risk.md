# Reputational Risk

## Reputational Risk

* **Partnership Risks**
  * **Scandal Involving Partners**
    * partnering with organizations that are later involved in scandals or controversies&#x20;
* **Social Impacts**
  * **Environmental Concerns**
    * energy-intensive nature of some blockchain networks
  * [**Inequitable**](#user-content-fn-1)[^1] **Access**
    * if blockchain solutions exclude certain segments of society due to technological barriers or lack of access
* **Public Perception**
  * **Misunderstanding of Technology**
    * complex/difficult for the general public to understand
    * misinterpretations/misconceptions → skepticism/mistrust
  * **Hype and Overpromising**
    * exaggerated claims about capabilities/benefits → disappointment if expectations are not met
  * **Association with Cryptocurrencies**
    * challenges in differentiating use of blockchain in companies from controversial aspects of cryptocurrencies (volatility, illegal activities)
* **Privacy and Security Concerns**
  * **Data Breaches**
    * mishandling private keys/vulnerabilities → data breaches → erode trust in the technology's security
  * **Loss of Anonymity**
    * in some systems,  pseudonymous transactions —  but not fully anonymous
    * if users' identities are mistakenly revealed or their privacy is compromised → reputational damage
* **Legal and Regulatory Issues**
  * **Non-Compliance**
    * solutions that do not adhere to relevant regulations/legal requirements  → legal actions or regulatory scrutiny
  * **Negative Associations**
    * if blockchain technology is perceived as facilitating illegal activities (e.g: money laundering) due to its pseudonymous nature
* **Transition Challenges**
  * **Disruptions**
    * integrating blockchain into existing processes → disruptions, downtimes, errors → affects business operations & customer experiences
