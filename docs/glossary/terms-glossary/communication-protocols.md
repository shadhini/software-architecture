# Communication Protocols

<table><thead><tr><th width="152.94921875" valign="top">Term</th><th>Definition</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td valign="top">TCP/IP</td><td><p>The fundamental suite of communication protocols used to connect devices on the internet and most private networks. </p><p>TCP/IP specifies how data should be </p><ul><li>packetized, </li><li>addressed, </li><li>transmitted, </li><li>routed, and </li><li>received at the destination.</li></ul><p>Together, TCP/IP forms the backbone of the internet and most modern computer networks.</p><p></p><p><strong>TCP (Transmission Control Protocol):</strong></p><ul><li>Ensures reliable and <strong>ordered delivery</strong> of data between applications.</li><li>Handles <strong>error checking</strong> and guarantees that data packets are delivered in the correct <strong>order</strong>.</li></ul><p><strong>IP (Internet Protocol):</strong></p><ul><li>Responsible for <strong>addressing</strong> and <strong>routing</strong> packets of data so that they can travel across networks and <strong>arrive</strong> at the correct <strong>destination</strong>.</li></ul><p></p></td></tr><tr><td valign="top"><code>HTTP</code>: HyperText Transfer Protocol</td><td><p>Protocol used on the internet to serve up content to anyone who requests it</p><p></p></td></tr><tr><td valign="top"><code>HTTPS</code>: HyperText Transfer Protocol Secured </td><td></td></tr></tbody></table>
